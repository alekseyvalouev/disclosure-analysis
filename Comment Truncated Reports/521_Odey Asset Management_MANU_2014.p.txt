To hedge fund manager Crispin Odey, it’s clear who is to blame for Manchester United’s decline
this season, and it’s not David Moyes, who left his post as manager of the English soccer club on
Tuesday.


Mr. Odey, one of London’s richest and most successful traders, has been running a short
position in the stock, revealed in December, and sees the club’s on-the-field problems as the
result of underinvestment over a number of years.


“The Glazers (the club’s American owners) have to be a little bit scared if revenues start
slipping… In the arms race they’ve underinvested,” said Odey.
