Can Chanos finally turn the bears on
Seagate, Western Digital?
May 9, 2013, 12:50 PM ET


Shares of both Seagate Technology and Western Digital fell on Thursday, a
reaction to negative comments made about the hard-disk business by Jim Chanos
at the Ira Sohn Conference the previous afternoon.


But those tracking the sector may wonder what took the legendary short-seller so
long to notice. Both hard-drive makers have been on a tear since the first of the
year — even though many sell-side brokers began turning down their ratings on the
stocks during the fall.


Since mid-October, Seagate   has seen at least seven analysts downgrade its
shares, while at least five have done the same on Western Digital , according to
data from Thomson Reuters.


At present, 84% of the brokers covering Seagate rate the stock as neutral or sell,
while 64% of those covering Western Digital have less-than-bullish views on the
stock.


Still, the stocks have largely shrugged off those warnings. In fact, both Seagate and
Western Digital shares set fresh 10-year highs on Wednesday before Chanos took
the stage to make his presentation at the conference.


\"\"


Chanos used his presentation to highlight the widely known weakness in the PC
sector, and argue for the fact that Seagate and Western will see their businesses
suffer more than investors seem to believe. He dismissed the argument that growth
in servers and cloud-based storage services will offset the sharp drop in PC sales.
He also targeted Seagate specifically, noting high rates of insider sales and
executive departures at the company.


Joe Yoo of Citigroup sounded a similar note of caution back on Oct. 12, when he
downgraded Seagate and Western Digital to sell ratings from his previous buy call.
He noted that the drop-off in PC sales was not being offset by sales on the
enterprise storage side. He said then that he might become more positive on the
group if they made \”a more aggressive push into enterprise SSDs\” (solid-state
drives). He still rates both stocks as a sell.


Seagate shares were down 5% by midday Thursday while Western Digital was
down 3.7%.


– Dan Gallagher
