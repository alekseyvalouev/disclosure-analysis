More Pressure For The HCI Group: Everybody Hates Takeouts
Oct. 15, 2015 5:26 AM ET10 comments
by: RH Analytics


Summary


Recent media reports suggest that the takeout business model may be pressured
further going forward.


There is a legislative movement afoot to curtail the takeout process, which has been
characterized as misleading and unfair to consumers.


Consumers are increasingly wary of takeouts as evidenced by a soaring opt-out rate
- over 50% of consumers are rejecting takeout offers, opting to remain with Citizens.


Two recent articles raise further doubts regarding the longevity of the takeout business
model for Florida insurers, such as the HCI Group (NYSE:HCI). There is a movement in
the Florida legislature to curtail takeouts. Additionally, recent statistics indicate that
consumer rejection rates for takeout offers are skyrocketing.


Florida lawmakers square off over downsizing Citizens Insurance discusses a bill currently
working through the Florida legislature. The purpose of it is to curtail Citizens's ability to
depopulate. The bill would require uniform takeout notices to increase clarity and would
outright ban automatic takeouts. The bill's sponsor said many aspects of the process are
"tricky, deceptive, misleading and unfair".


The article also notes that many Florida lawmakers believe that Governor Scott's support
of takeouts is a "payback for campaign contributions". While we would agree that the
takeout process is highly politicized and at times appears to have a quid pro quo element,
we suspect the Governor's office is not the only beneficiary.


A short but interesting article in a Florida real estate trade publication points out recent
changes in takeout statistics. The article notes that the opt-out rate, or the rate at which
consumers reject takeouts to remain with Citizens, has increased dramatically over the
last year. In 2014, approximately 30% of consumers subject to a takeout offer opted to
remain with Citizens. In 2015, that figure has increased to over 50%.
